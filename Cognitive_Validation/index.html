Cognitive Data Validation

Step 1: read the conversation and answer the 3 questions on your own. Step 2: the gold answer is revealed and you rate the gold and the distractors.

Annotation Guidelines

Each conversation has two steps. Step 1 (answer): choose, on your own, the option that best captures the persuadee's state in the final utterance — the gold answer is hidden. Step 2 (rate): the gold is revealed; rate the gold and the distractors.

The three aspects
  • Belief — an internal representation of what the persuadee considers true.
  • Emotion — the persuadee's feeling state, conveyed by cues (a short phrase).
  • Intent — the persuadee's goal, motivation, or underlying reason for what they say.
A good gold answer should…
  • Reflect the cognitive state expressed in the final utterance only — not earlier turns.
  • Capture the underlying state, not the literal content — no quotes or restating what was said.
  • Stay at the right abstraction level: if the utterance were worded differently, it should still apply.
  • Be compact (emotion = a short phrase).

Rate the gold (1–5): how well it captures the true state at the final utterance.

1 = completely wrong · 5 = fully captures the true state
A good distractor should…
  • Be definitely wrong, yet plausible and context-bound (no obvious "shortcut" that's impossible).
  • Not be "technically correct" or too close to the true state; not imply mean behavior; not quote the dialogue.
  • Match the style/length of the gold answer.

Rate the distractors overall (1–5): how good they are as distractors.

1 = bad distractors · 5 = good distractors (plausible but definitely wrong)
